# STAR Presentation

## S — Situation
Students rely on part-time work to pay for tuition and living costs. Inflation reduces their purchasing power, making essentials harder to afford.

## T — Task
Answer the question: Have student wages kept up with inflation over the past 10 years? Viewers should be able to see trends in real vs nominal wages.

## A — Action
- Built an interactive chart comparing nominal vs real wages.
- Added a year-range slider to explore trends over time.
- Processed raw wage and CPI data to calculate real wages.

## R — Result
- Real wages have remained mostly flat, despite nominal wage increases.
- Interaction shows that inflation erodes the gains in nominal wages over time.
- Limitation: Only U.S. data is included; may not represent all student demographics.
- Key takeaway: Students cannot rely solely on wage increases to keep up with inflation.
